Need urgent help?

If you feel like you may harm yourself or someone else, seek immediate help from local emergency services, a trusted adult, guardian, friend, or the nearest hospital.

Talk to Someone You Trust

Speaking with someone you trust can help reduce emotional pressure. This could be a friend, family member, mentor, counselor, or support worker.

Grounding Techniques

  • Take 5 slow deep breaths.
  • Name 5 things you can see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear.
  • Sit somewhere quiet and drink water slowly.
  • Write down what you are feeling without judging yourself.

Healthy Coping Ideas

  • Take a short walk or stretch your body.
  • Listen to calming music.
  • Take a break from stressful screens or noise.
  • Rest, journal, or talk to someone supportive.

When to Seek Professional Help

  • If low mood continues for many days or weeks.
  • If stress starts affecting school, sleep, or daily life.
  • If you feel overwhelmed often and cannot cope alone.
  • If you have thoughts of self-harm or hopelessness.
